EXPERIMENT

    * find more info on this experiment on the LEAVENING AGENTS page! *
  • PURPOSE: to determine which leavening agent (no yeast, yeast, yeast w/ honey, sourdough starter, baking soda, baking powder) produces the highest rise of bread dough and is most efficient.
  • HYPOTHESIS: the dough made using yeast activated with honey will have the highest overall rise, as the addition of glucose for the yeast to feed on will accelerate the fermentation process.
  • MATERIALS:
    • 6 ・ 3 ½ cups flour
    • 6 ・1 tbsp sugar
    • 6 ・1 tbsp salt
    • 2 ・7 g yeast
    • 6 ・300 mL water at 30° C
    • tbsp measuring spoon
    • 6 graduated plastic containers
    • 6 mixing spoons/spatulas
    • 6 baking pans
  • PROCEDURE:
    1. In a large bowl, combine warm water, yeast, salt, oil, sugar and 4 cups of flour. Mix thoroughly and let dough rise until doubled in size.
    2. Gradually add about 4 cups flour, kneading until smooth. Place dough in a greased bowl and turn several times to coat. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise until doubled in size.
    3. Punch down the dough and let it rest a few minutes. Divide into 3 equal parts, shape each into a loaf and place in a bread pan. Let rise until doubled in size.
    4. Bake at 350˚ for 35-45 minutes.
  • RESULTS:

    As predicted, the loaf with yeast + honey as the leavening agent (#3) had the highest rise. The next highest was #2, the bread made with yeast, followed by sourdough. This makes sense as for all three of these processes, what occurred was fermentation. Next was the combination of baking soda and citric acid, which rose the same amount as the loaf with baking powder. Finally, the bread with no leavening agents had a negligible rise and could nearly be considered inedible.
