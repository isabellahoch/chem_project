learning goals · enduring understandings

learning goals

  • 8.1 The physical properties of hydrocarbons and other organic molecules depend on their chemical structures and ability to engage in intermolecular attractions. (many of the substances involved in breadmaking are organic molecules)
  • 6.3 The unique ability of hydrogen ions to participate in polar covalent bonds but also to ionize and transfer between chemicals leads to acid-base reactions. (the chemical processes for baking soda and baking powder are acid-base reactions)
  • 5.3 Because solutions are mixtures (not pure substances), they can be made with varying amounts of solute and solvent. (experimenting with different concentrations of ingredients in baking; dissolving yeast/flour in water)

enduring understandings

  • #3: Chemistry can be understood and described at the macroscopic (observable) level, the submicroscopic (particle) level, and the symbolic level. Making connections between these three is crucial to developing true understanding of chemical phenomena.
  • #5: Chemical reactions require particle collisions to occur, in the correct orientation and with sufficient energy. This means that reaction conditions can be manipulated to change the rate of the reaction.
